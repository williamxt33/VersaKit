Coin Flip Information

What is a Coin Flip?

A coin flip is a simple random event with two equally likely outcomes: heads or tails.

Probability: Each flip has exactly 50% chance for heads and 50% for tails

Independence: Each flip is independent - previous results don't affect future flips

Common Uses

Decision Making: Simple yes/no choices

Sports: Starting possession in games

Games: Random selection or turn order

Probability: Teaching basic statistics

Interesting Facts

• The longest recorded streak is 76 consecutive heads

• Flipping 10 heads in a row has 0.098% probability

• Law of Large Numbers: More flips = closer to 50/50

• Coin flips are used in cryptography

Statistics Tips

• Small samples can show extreme results

• 100+ flips typically show 45-55% split

• Streaks are normal in random events

• Past results don't predict future flips
