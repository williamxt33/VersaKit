ROI Information

What is ROI?

Return on Investment (ROI) measures the profitability of an investment relative to its cost.

Simple ROI: (Final Value - Initial Investment) / Initial Investment × 100

Annualized ROI: Takes into account the time period of the investment

Real ROI: Adjusts for inflation to show true purchasing power gains

ROI Benchmarks

Excellent: 20%+ annual return

Good: 10-20% annual return

Average: 5-10% annual return (stock market average ~10%)

Below Average: Less than 5% annual return

Types of Investments

Stocks: Historically 10% average annual return

Bonds: Typically 3-5% annual return

Real Estate: 8-12% average annual return

Savings Accounts: 0.5-2% annual return

High-Yield Savings: 4-5% annual return

Investment Tips

• Diversify your investment portfolio

• Consider the time value of money

• Factor in all costs and fees

• Account for taxes and inflation

• Compare ROI against market benchmarks

• Review and rebalance regularly
