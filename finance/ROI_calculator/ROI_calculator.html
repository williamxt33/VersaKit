About Return on Investment (ROI)

What is ROI?

Return on Investment (ROI) is a key performance metric used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment. It measures the profitability by comparing the net gain or loss relative to the investment's cost.

Simple ROI Formula:
ROI = [(Final Value - Initial Investment) / Initial Investment] × 100%

Annualized ROI Formula:
AROI = [(1 + ROI)^(1/n) - 1] × 100%
where n = number of years

Real ROI: Adjusts for inflation to show true purchasing power gains

Understanding ROI Components

Initial Investment: Total starting capital including:

  • Purchase price
  • Transaction costs
  • Setup/installation costs
  • Training or onboarding expenses

Final Value Components:

  • Sale price or current value
  • Dividends or rental income
  • Cost savings or revenue increase
  • Tax benefits or depreciation

Investment Performance Benchmarks

Stocks:

  • S&P 500: ~10% historical average
  • Growth stocks: 15-25% potential
  • Dividend stocks: 2-6% yield plus growth

Real Estate:

  • Residential: 8-12% total return
  • Commercial: 6-12% cap rate
  • REITs: 8-15% total return

Business/Projects:

  • Small business: 15-30% target
  • Marketing campaigns: 5:1 ratio goal
  • Equipment/Technology: 10-25% efficiency gain
