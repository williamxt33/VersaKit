Compound Interest Information

What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest calculated on both the initial principal and accumulated interest from previous periods.

Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where A = final amount, P = principal, r = rate, n = compounds per year, t = time in years

Compounding Frequencies

Annually: Interest compounds once per year

Quarterly: Interest compounds 4 times per year

Monthly: Interest compounds 12 times per year

Daily: Interest compounds 365 times per year

Regular Contributions

Adding regular contributions significantly boosts growth over time through dollar-cost averaging.

Start of Period: Contributions earn interest for the full period

End of Period: Contributions are added at period end

Investment Tips

• Start investing early to maximize compound growth

• Regular contributions accelerate wealth building

• Higher compounding frequency increases returns

• Consider inflation when planning long-term goals

• Reinvest dividends and interest for optimal growth
